# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Board members, planning directors, destination operators, developers, transport authorities, architects, and operations teams evaluating difficult access problems. They need one shared, bounded view of door-to-door time, transfers, terminal friction, community limits, energy assumptions, and resilience before funding a larger technical program.

## Product Purpose

Avenor Passage Systems is a fictional Zürich mobility-planning studio. This showcase demonstrates how a Corridor Opportunity Study can turn a complex regional access question into a clear board decision. It models the wholly synthetic region `APS-SR-01`, endpoints `E1` and `E2`, corridors `C1` and `C2`, and exactly three operating states. Those neutral identifiers are mandatory across visible UI, accessible naming, exports, print, and filenames. It never presents a real route, operating service, approval, forecast, or engagement channel.

Success means visitors understand that time, not distance, is the primary terrain; can compare the three states without relying on color or canvas; can inspect assumptions and uncertainty beside every measured-looking value; and can qualify a prototype study while receiving an explicit confirmation that nothing was submitted to an operating company.

## Brand Personality

Civic, lucid, measured. The emotional register is informed confidence rather than spectacle. Avenor should feel like a careful planning team working around a sunlit cartographic table, testing access and limits rather than admiring a vehicle.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble dark science-fiction control rooms, neon contour maps, glossy mobility launch films, glass dashboards, floating chrome vehicles, generic luxury hospitality pages, or editorial-template portfolios. Avoid gradients, bloom, particles, fog, dramatic countdowns, fabricated client credibility, and open-ended route calculator language.

## Design Principles

1. **Time is the terrain.** Make access duration and transfer friction spatially understandable before discussing technology.
2. **Evidence stays beside the claim.** Place assumptions, uncertainty, fictional status, and limitations next to every metric.
3. **The map is a civic instrument.** Use direct labels, stable legends, tables, and tactile planning conventions rather than cinematic effects.
4. **Enhancement never owns meaning.** Semantic HTML and state-specific SVG contours are the complete, default 2D experience. The contours and optional registered Three.js relief are generated from the same synthetic modeled-minute surface. Relief is opt-in at every viewport and never replaces the canonical identifiers, routes, boundary, or evidence.
5. **Commercial clarity without false promise.** Explain scope, indicative fictional price, process, and exclusions plainly. Forms submit nowhere and confirm that no operating company received a request.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA intent with semantic landmarks, a skip link, visible focus, keyboard and touch operation, 44px targets, direct labels, non-color encodings, an accessible table, screen-reader announcements, forced-colors support, print support, and robust 200 percent zoom behavior. Reduced motion removes terrain morphing, camera travel, scrubbed movement, and continuous rendering. Mobile defaults to the complete 2D experience. No-canvas is canonical, not a degraded apology.
